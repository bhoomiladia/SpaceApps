Landsat Missions Overview

The Landsat program, initiated in 1972, has been capturing detailed Earth imagery for environmental monitoring, land use change analysis, and climate studies. Over time, a series of satellites equipped with progressively more advanced sensors have been launched. Each Landsat mission has contributed significantly to global remote sensing applications.

Key Features of Landsat Missions

  • Multispectral Scanner (MSS): The earliest sensor used in Landsat 1-5. Captured data in four spectral bands with a spatial resolution of 80 meters.
  • Thematic Mapper (TM): Introduced on Landsat 4, this sensor captured data in seven spectral bands with a spatial resolution of 30 meters (visible, near-infrared, and shortwave infrared) and 120 meters for thermal infrared.
  • Enhanced Thematic Mapper Plus (ETM+): Added on Landsat 7, providing 30-meter resolution in most spectral bands and a 15-meter panchromatic band for high-resolution imagery.
  • Operational Land Imager (OLI): Introduced on Landsat 8, capturing data in nine spectral bands with improved signal-to-noise performance and a new coastal/aerosol band.
  • Thermal Infrared Sensor (TIRS): Captures thermal infrared data at a resolution of 100 meters for temperature and heat analysis.

Notable Achievements of the Landsat Program

  • Landsat 5 holds the record for the longest-operating Earth observation satellite, operational for nearly 29 years.
  • Landsat 7 ETM+ introduced the panchromatic band, enabling higher spatial resolution for urban and land use studies.
  • Landsat 8 and Landsat 9 continue to provide high-quality multispectral and thermal imagery for environmental research and monitoring.
  • The Landsat program’s 50-year archive offers a comprehensive and consistent view of Earth’s surface, supporting long-term studies on climate change, deforestation, and agricultural expansion.

Applications of Landsat Data

  • Environmental Monitoring: Assessing changes in forest cover, urban expansion, and agricultural practices over time.
  • Climate Change Studies: Analyzing long-term temperature changes, ice melt, and sea level rise using thermal infrared and other spectral data.
  • Water Resource Management: Monitoring water bodies, detecting changes in water quality, and managing irrigation.
  • Disaster Response: Assessing damage from wildfires, floods, hurricanes, and other natural disasters using satellite imagery.
